The unknown story of the Olympics

The first recorded Olympic Games took place in 776 BC in a place called Olympia, in Greece. But the Games were not created to celebrate sport. They were created to honour the Greek god Zeus. The early Olympics had little variety compared to today. In fact, for the first many years, there was only one event: a short running race.

According to ancient stories, the Games were founded by a man named Heracles. He was a great athlete who wanted to create a peaceful competition. People would walk for many days to get to Olympia. They would camp in simple tents, and the aroma of cooking food filled the air. The athletes were special. They had to train for ten months before the Games. They believed in proper nutrition to make their bodies strong. They didn't have many luxuries. This was a Greek tradition.

The biggest difficulty for the ancient Games was not the fighting, but politics and war. Greece was not one country; it was many small cities that often fought each other. To allow athletes and visitors to travel safely to Olympia, they created an "Olympic Truce". For one month before the Games, all wars had to stop.

From a simple race to honour a god, to a global celebration of peace and sport, the Olympics have passed through fire, war and peace.
