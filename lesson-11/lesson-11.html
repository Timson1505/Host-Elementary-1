The death dealer

Have you ever heard of the Nobel Prize? It’s the most famous award in the world, given to people who help humanity. But the man behind it, Alfred Nobel, had a dark nickname: the “death dealer.” Why? Let’s explore his life.

Alfred Nobel was born in Stockholm, Sweden, in 1833. As a child, he was often ill. He suffered from nausea, dizziness, and weakness. Because of his poor health, he rarely played outside. Instead, he studied at home. His family could not pay for good health insurance, so his mother used simple first aid and bandages to help him. Because he was so sick, he had a lack of energy for sports, but his mind was sharp.

Alfred was a brilliant chemist. During his life he invented 350 products, but his most famous invention was dynamite. It was very powerful and dangerous. Because of dynamite, many people called him the “death dealer”. This nickname made him feel sad and guilty because he never wanted to hurt people. He regretted his invention when his brother died in a dynamite explosion which he and it was used in wars and caused many deaths. He wanted to finance outstanding people who worked for peace and knowledge Because of his guilt about dynamite, he decided to reward those who prevent wars and save lives.
Nobel had a disease — he suffered from heart attack and dizziness. In France, some newspapers blamed him for betray because they thought he sold dynamits to Russia.

Then, Nobel decided to use his own money to reward outstanding people and scientists. He created the Nobel Prizes to recognize achievements in peace, science, and literature. He wanted to help the world because he believed that knowledge and good ideas could make life better.

The big dream in a short limited life

He changed the way we listen to music, use a phone and even think about computers. He was fired from his own company. He invented and helped to invent more than 300 patents.

Steve Jobs was born in 1955 in San Francisco, US. His biological parents gave him up for adoption. His adoptive parents were not rich, young Steve learned the value of hard work early. As a child, he often felt nausea and dizziness. His family did not have health insurance, so his mother used a simple first aid. Because of a lack of interest in traditional school, he was often bored. But it led him to explore electronics in his garage. Steve Jobs did not invent the first computer. But together with a partner, he built the first Apple computer in his parents' garage in 1976. People loved it because it was small and easy to use. Later, he created (1984), iPod (2001), the iPhone (2007) and the iPad (2010). In 1985, he was fired from his own company - Apple.

In 2003, doctors discovered that Steve had a rare form of cancer. Because of his belief in natural medicine, he refused the surgery for nine months - it was his mistake. The cancer grew, because he waited too long. He often felt dizy and the blood pressure dropped dangaerously. He needed surgery to remove some part of his organs. He had to wear a bandage on stomach for a couple of months. So, he became extremely thin.

Steve Jobs was a billionaire, but he lived simply. He never bought a giant yacht. Instead he used his money to finance brilliant artists, designers and creative big dreamers. He strongly believed that creativity could change the world. Because of adoption, sometimes he felt a lack of belonging. So, he built a "family" of talented people at Apple. He also financed Pixar - the animation studio.

Steve Jobs died in 2011, at the age of 56. He accepted his death because he knew he had done what he loved. Once he said: "Your time is limited. Do not waste it living someone else's life". He only regretted not having more time. His story teaches that health is fragile, but a great idea can live forever.
