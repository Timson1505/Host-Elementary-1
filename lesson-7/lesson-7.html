My trip to Italy

Last year, I went to Rome on my holiday. Also, I visited my dream country, the Vatican. I booked my hotel in advance for a week, so I had a lot of time to explore the culture, people and sightseeings. It was my first time in Rome, and the adventure started right at the airport. It seemed I arrived right at the museum after passport control. There were many art pictures, sculptures and audio history. I arrived in Rome at midnight and took a metro to the city center. My hotel is located in the center of Rome and it was so beautiful, I felt like I lived as a Roman Imperor. I was excited to see many beautiful places there.

In the morning I was in a rush to explore the city after breakfast. The first place I wished to see was "Colloseum" where gladiators used to fight. I decided to take a walk. There are so many small cars, because there are many narrow streets. There almost no traffic lights and sidewalks in old the town amoung the narrow streets. I took a lot of pictures and talked to local people, they were so kind.

I traveled alone, so I did everything I wanted. I decided to try real Italian pasta and pizza. I found a cozy cafe on the top of the glass building which opens a beautiful view to the night city. It's located in front of the historical museum along the big avenue. I saw a park behind the building.

It seems everyone walks outside in the evening, no one stays at home, even pets. That was a very nice atmosphere to relax. People were singing, dancing and painting. I was walking and smiling amoung that crowded people enjoying my holiday.

By the way, I took part in opening ceremony of the "Kyrgyz Park" in the center of Rome. Now I know, one week is not enough to explore Rome and the Vatican. I fell in love in Rome and I definitely will come again. Now, I believe - "My dreams come true".

My dream country

I had a dream to visit the smallest country in the world, today my dream came true. After Rome I am going to Vatican. This country is located in Rome. As soon as I arrived, I stepped on the sidewalk outside St. Peter's Square. The square itself is enormous.

Before I heard only one person can enter the Vatican's library amoung the over 8 billion people in the world, and it's the Roman Pope. I walked following the road sign near a busy crossroad. At the traffic light, I waited carefully before using the crosswalk to reach the entrence. First, I had to take a metro from my hotel, then change at another stop. Inside the museum, paintings and sculptures filled every room. After, I visited the Sistine Chapel. I stood in the middle of the room looking up at masterpiece on the ceiling. I wanted to remember every detail, so I walked slowly along the walls, carefully not to block other visitors.

Finally, I explored the Vatican Gardens. I walked straight down a long path lined with trees, then turned left to a small fountain. There was a small pharmacy that looked like a secret place behind the main street.

By the end of the day, I walked countless corners, crossroads and sidewalks, yet every step was something new for me. The Vatican is magical and I am lucky to experience its beauty.
