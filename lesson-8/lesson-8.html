Adidas VS Puma

The story of Adidas and Puma is one of the greatest family dramas in business history. It is the story of two choices and what happened because of them.

There were two brothers in the small German town, Adolf and Rudolf Daslers. They had the ability to make the best athletes shoes in the world. In their mother's washroom, they started a company. Adolf or "Adi" was an inventor. Rudolf or "Rudi" was a salesman. Their experience was perfect. Adi could design shoes that could help athletes could run faster. Rudi could sell their dream to the world.

They had the freedom to innovate. They used the 1936 Olympic Games as an Opportunity. They made shoes for the American runner Jesse Owens. When he won four gold medals, the world noticed them, and their confidence grew.

But then, a challenge came. During World War 2 they had a misunderstanding, then they made a final choice. In 1948, they split the company in two. Adolf Dasler based "adidas", and Rudolf based "puma".

But, what if they could work together? What if Adolf and Rudolf brothers could design together?

Imagine if they used their shared responibility to their brand, they could solve any challenge twice as fast. Adi's genius for design and Rudi's genius for for sales could be an unstoppable ability.

They could use their experience to improve not just shoes, but whole sports. They could face every competitor with double confidence. The opportunity they lost could be an opportunity to build the greatest sports company in the world.

Sometimes, the big challenge to solve is not in the market, but it could be at home. The wall they built in a small town in Germany kept them out from their own greatest possible future.

The story of "Blue Ribbon".

It doesn't matter where you live. But I am sure you can find my company's brand, "Nike".
Phil Knight's experience as a runner gave him an idea and he made a choice. He formed a small company - "Blue Ribbon Sports". The main idea was - to support American athlets, runners, and import shoes from Japan.

The early challenge was just survival. Selling shoes from the car trunk, but the small Blue Ribbon Team had the freedom. Every problem was a chance to improve. Their main ability was a partnership: Knight's vision paired with his coach and business partner Bill Bowerman's skill in design. Together, they believed they could solve the problem of imperfect shoe.

For years, Blue Ribbon succeeded. So, the opportunity was rising to create their own line of sneakers.

With a big opportunity, they made a risky choice: To change the name and produce their own shoes, not import from Japan. A key opportunity was when they chose a young athlete named Michael Jordan, Nike saw potential to support a legend. So, Nike started producing the brand shoes devoted to "Jordan" - jordan sneakers.

Nike's story is about the Responibility to serve the athletes. It is the story of how a simple choice, the freedom to innovate and the skill to adapt, could grow from a crazy idea into a global symbol of a human potential. The challenge never ends, it will countinue with every step.
